# FoodHub Order Analysis

**Type:** Exploratory Data Analysis  
**Tools:** Python, Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn

## Problem
FoodHub lacked clarity on which cuisines, restaurants, and time patterns were driving order volume, making it difficult to prioritize marketing spend and partnership decisions.

## Approach
Analyzed 1,898 food delivery orders across cuisine type, order cost, delivery time, day of week, customer rating, and restaurant. Cleaned data, handled missing ratings, and built visualizations to surface behavioral patterns across each dimension.

## Key Findings
- American and Japanese cuisines drove 55%+ of all orders
- 71.2% of orders were placed on weekends
- Weekend delivery averaged 22.5 min vs. 28.3 min on weekdays — 26% faster
- Top 10 restaurants accounted for 46.7% of total order volume
- Spanish cuisine had the highest avg customer rating at 4.83/5

## Recommendations
- Focus weekend promotions on American and Japanese cuisines
- Investigate weekday delivery delays — a 26% gap is significant
- Prioritize partnership expansion with Shake Shack and The Meatball Shop
- Promote Spanish and Indian cuisines — highest satisfaction scores
